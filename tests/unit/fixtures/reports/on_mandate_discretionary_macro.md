The macro regime is shifting from "higher for longer" toward a Fed easing cycle, triggered by softening CPI and a cooling labour market. Within that regime, long-duration assets and rate-sensitive sectors — XLU, XLRE, TLT as proxies — win, while financials and cyclicals face headwinds.

Regime narrative: Core PCE has decelerated for four consecutive months. The 2-year / 10-year yield curve, which was inverted at -100bps six months ago, has steepened to -20bps — the classic early-cycle signal that the Fed is approaching the pivot. Fed funds futures are pricing two 25bp cuts in the next 12 months. The business cycle is in late-expansion / early-contraction: ISM Manufacturing has been below 50 for three months, but ISM Services is still above 50, consistent with a soft-landing baseline.

Expression in equities: In this regime, long-duration equities (high-multiple growth, utilities, REITs) historically outperform as real rates fall. Financials compress as the yield curve flattens further before steepening. XLU and XLRE are the clearest regime expressions; within individual names, AMT (American Tower, rate-sensitive REIT) and NEE (NextEra Energy) benefit directly from lower long-end rates.

The names here are selected as regime expressions, not because of their individual fundamentals. The macro thesis is the position; the equities are the vehicle.
