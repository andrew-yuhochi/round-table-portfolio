## Thesis

XLU, XLRE, and TLT are buys because the Fed is pivoting and the macro regime
favours duration assets.

## Rationale

**XLU** (Utilities ETF): The Fed pivot is coming and utilities are the
primary beneficiary.  Core PCE is decelerating; the yield curve is steepening.
In this late-cycle macro regime, rate-sensitive sectors outperform.  The
business-cycle phase — late expansion, approaching pivot — historically favours
utilities, REITs, and long-duration bonds.

**XLRE** (Real Estate ETF): Real estate benefits directly from falling long
rates.  As the Fed cuts, cap rates compress and REIT valuations re-rate
upward.  The macro thesis is the position.  The yield curve is telling you
to be in rate-sensitive assets.

**TLT** (20+ Year Treasury ETF): The regime is shifting from hawkish to dovish.
CPI is below 3%, PCE is decelerating, and the labour market is softening.
Long-duration bonds are the cleanest expression of this regime thesis.  The
Fed will cut; TLT will rally.

## Evidence

Sourced from: FRED macro series (PCE, CPI, yield curve), RSS feeds for Fed
communications.
