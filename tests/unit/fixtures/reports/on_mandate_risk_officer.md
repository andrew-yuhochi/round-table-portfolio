The proposed panel portfolio has three concentration risks that must be addressed before Week 1 positions are sized: technology sector overweight, correlated drawdown exposure in the growth cluster, and insufficient tail-risk coverage given the macro backdrop.

Technology concentration: Six of the twelve shortlisted names are in technology or technology-adjacent sectors (semiconductors, cloud, software). Combined weight at equal sizing is 50% — well above the 30% sector cap in the risk framework. Correlation within the cluster is high (average pairwise correlation 0.72 over the past 12 months). A single sector shock (e.g. AI spending pullback, export-control escalation) would produce simultaneous drawdown across half the portfolio. Recommendation: trim the tech cluster to ≤4 names or cap individual weights to achieve ≤30% sector exposure.

Correlated drawdown — growth cluster: NVDA, CRWD, and PANW share a positive correlation of 0.65+. In the 2022 drawdown, all three declined 30–65% over a 9-month period. The VaR contribution of this cluster at proposed sizing is disproportionate: 38% of total portfolio VaR from 25% of names. Recommendation: reduce sizing on at least one of these three names.

Tail coverage: The portfolio has no meaningful short-duration or defensive allocation. Under a stress scenario (S&P -25%, VIX spike to 35), the portfolio's estimated drawdown is -32% — within tolerance, but the downside is not distributed across factor types. PG and JNJ (defensive quality names proposed by the quant persona) would materially improve the tail profile.
