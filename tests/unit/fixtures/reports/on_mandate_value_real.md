METHOD THIS WEEK (first week — empty memory). I bought businesses, not tickers: screened well-known large-cap S&P 500 names I suspected traded below conservative intrinsic value, pulled fundamentals as core evidence, confirmed each gap with a current quote, and used WebSearch to read filings/guidance behind anomalous multiples. With price-history tools (prenarrow/prices/technicals) down this week I lost nothing — I ignore price-chart momentum by mandate. Examined 13 names across pharma, financials, telecom/cable, energy, tech: CVS, PFE, CSCO, C, BAC, CMCSA, VZ, CVX, MRK, GILD, BMY, T. Four made the shortlist.

MERCK (MRK) — highest conviction. $115.65, ~11x forward P/E, 78.1% gross margin, 17.9% ROE, D/E 0.94, ~$18B FCF, 3.2% yield. The discount is Keytruda patent-cliff fear (>50% of sales, US expiry end-2028). Market capitalizes a known dated event as permanent impairment. Buying a 78%-gross-margin franchise at a low-teens multiple, pipeline free. Net debt rising toward ~$50B post-Cidara/Terns is the watch-item, comfortable against ~$27B EBITDA.

COMCAST (CMCSA) — cheapest on owner earnings once headline corrected. 4.4x trailing P/E artifact of one-time $7.07B Q2-2025 gain; normalized ~9-10x. ~$20B/yr FCF, EV/EBITDA 4.9x, 19.8% ROE, 70.1% gross margin, D/E 1.0, at $24.85 just above $24.13 52-wk low. Broadband erosion the legitimate bear case; low-single-digit EV/EBITDA on a durable cash machine is the margin of safety.

VERIZON (VZ) — bond-like cash stream priced for no growth. 11.6x P/E at $47.87, 16.7% ROE, 58.9% gross margin, EV/EBITDA 7.7x, beta 0.22, well-covered dividend. Leverage (D/E 1.5) the only blemish, serviced by stable EBITDA.

PFIZER (PFE) — statistically cheapest at ~8.5-9x forward ($25.55 vs reaffirmed 2026 adj EPS $2.80-3.00), 74.8% gross margin, D/E 0.75, 6.6% yield. Margin-of-safety risk: FY25 FCF $9.08B did NOT cover $9.77B dividends, 2026 absorbs ~$3B headwinds. Cheap if FCF normalizes; value trap if dividend cut. Smallest sizing, widest required margin.

WHAT I PASSED ON. CITIGROUP (C): 0.98x book / 15.7x P/E look value-y but at $131.26 pressed against $135 52-wk high — already re-rated, 7.55% ROE / 0.6% ROA on D/E 3.3 doesn't earn cost of capital. CHEVRON (CVX): 30.4x P/E on 6.23% ROE is trough-oil earnings dressed as cheap; won't underwrite a commodity cycle as intrinsic value. CVS (65.7x P/E, 3.87% ROE, current ratio 0.84) and CSCO (49.6x P/E, 5.7x book): distorted/elevated multiples, no defensible gap. GILD: excellent business (42% ROE, 79% margin) but 6.7x book / 18.7x earnings — quality without the discount. BAC: 12.2x P/E, 1.3x book, 10.5% ROE decent but unremarkable. BMY: 15.8x P/E and 41.6% ROE flatter an intangible-heavy, D/E 2.4 balance sheet.

OVERALL STANCE. Two structural value pockets: large-cap pharma de-rated on patent-cliff fear (MRK quality, PFE deep-but-risky) and telecom/cable priced as melting ice cubes despite ~$20B-scale FCF (CMCSA, VZ). Every call anchored to a numbers-defensible gap, not a story; wider safety where uncertainty is higher (PFE smallest, MRK largest). I expect to look dull in a momentum rally and vindicated in a drawdown — which is the point.
