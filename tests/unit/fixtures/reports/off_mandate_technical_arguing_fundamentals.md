## Thesis

MSFT, GOOG, and JNJ are buys based on their superior fundamentals, strong
balance sheets, and attractive FCF yields.

## Rationale

**MSFT** (Microsoft): FCF yield of 3.5% is modest but the quality of earnings
is exceptional.  The balance sheet is fortress-grade — net cash of $50bn.
Normalized P/E of 32× is fair for a business with this earnings quality and
competitive moat.  Azure growth is durable; the reinvestment runway into AI
infrastructure is long.

**GOOG** (Alphabet): FCF yield approaching 5% on a net-cash balance sheet.
The advertising business generates more free cash flow than most companies in
the S&P 500.  P/E of 22× is reasonable given the quality and diversification
of the earnings stream.  The valuation discount to MSFT is not justified by
the underlying business quality.

**JNJ** (Johnson & Johnson): Normalized P/E of 14× on a fortress balance sheet.
FCF yield of 5.5% with a 30-year dividend growth record.  Earnings quality is
extremely high — cash conversion above 90%.  The margin of safety at current
valuation is genuine.

Sourced from: fundamentals tool (FCF yield, P/E, net debt, earnings quality),
EDGAR 10-K filings.
