## Thesis

CVS, WBA, and T are compelling buys because they trade at steep discounts to
intrinsic value with substantial margins of safety.

## Rationale

**CVS** (CVS Health): The stock trades at 8× normalized earnings — a deep
discount to intrinsic value.  FCF yield of 9% is exceptional.  The balance
sheet is manageable.  Mean reversion alone justifies a buy at this multiple;
you don't need growth to make money here.

**WBA** (Walgreens): At 0.3× book value, the margin of safety is enormous.
Owner earnings are temporarily depressed but the intrinsic value of the
pharmacy network is well above the current price.  This is a classic
deep-value situation — unloved, cheap, and misunderstood.

**T** (AT&T): Normalized P/E of 7×, dividend yield of 6.5%.  The debt load
is large but manageable at current rates.  The stock is cheap on every
valuation metric — P/E, P/B, FCF yield.  There is no growth here, but you
are getting a lot of asset value for the price.
