## Thesis

Gold, TLT, and XLU are buys because the Fed is clearly pivoting and the
macro narrative strongly favours duration and defensive assets.

## Rationale

**Gold**: The Fed is going to cut rates.  Core PCE has been decelerating for
four months and the labor market is softening.  When the Fed cuts, real rates
fall and gold rallies.  This is the classic macro regime for gold.  The
narrative here is compelling — the Fed pivot story is well-supported and the
market is under-positioned for the dovish turn.

**TLT** (20+ Year Treasury ETF): The yield curve is steepening because the
market believes the Fed's next move is down.  This is a directional macro bet
on duration — when the Fed cuts, long-duration bonds rally.  The regime thesis
is clear: late cycle, slowing growth, Fed pivot incoming.  The story is strong
enough to override the negative carry on this trade.

**XLU** (Utilities ETF): Utilities are rate-sensitive and they will benefit
from the Fed pivot.  The sector has lagged YTD because of the higher-for-longer
narrative; that narrative is now reversing.  The regime shift from hawkish to
dovish is the key catalyst.

Sourced from: FRED macro series (PCE, yield curve, employment), Fed
communications via web search, RSS feeds for macro commentary.
