The multiple looks rich on trailing earnings but is reasonable on a 3-year forward view if the growth runway is as long as the platform evidence suggests. A long runway justifies a rich entry; this is not cheapness I'm paying for.

The valuation premium over legacy peers is justified by the platform cohesion and the switching-cost moat deepening with each new module adopted.

MNST (Monster Beverage): Revenue growth of 12% YoY into a global energy drink TAM that is still expanding. Distribution density via the Coca-Cola partnership gives Monster a compounding advantage that is hard to replicate. Gross margin is recovering post-commodity headwinds. This is a classic compounder: durable, reinvestable, with a long runway in emerging markets.
